Introduction

Agriculture, as a cornerstone of human civilization, has evolved to meet the ever-growing demand for food, fiber, and other essential products. At the heart of sustainable and productive agriculture lies the intricate relationship between crops and the underlying soil ecosystem. Soil biodiversity, encompassing the variety of living organisms within the soil, plays a pivotal role in maintaining soil health and fertility. From microscopic bacteria and fungi to earthworms and larger organisms, the diverse community of soil life contributes to nutrient cycling, soil structure improvement, and pest regulation. As we navigate the complexities of modern agriculture, understanding and nurturing soil biodiversity becomes paramount for fostering resilient and environmentally responsible farming practices. This dynamic interplay between agriculture and soil biodiversity forms the foundation for sustainable food production, environmental conservation, and the overall well-being of our planet. Agricultural biodiversity is crucial for ensuring food security and resilience in the face of environmental challenges. Diverse crops and livestock provide a genetic reservoir that enhances adaptability, strengthens ecosystem health, and supports sustainable agricultural practices. Bioversity International is dedicated to safeguarding and using agricultural biodiversity to improve people's lives.
